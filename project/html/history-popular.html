History & Popular Figures

Timeline of Tartu

(1030 - Middle Ages)

Early Beginnings

Tartu was first mentioned in 1030 as Yuryev, a settlement founded by Kievan Rus. During the Middle Ages, it became a vital trading hub as part of the Hanseatic League, known as Dorpat.

16th - 17th Century

Swedish and Polish Rule

Tartu changed hands between Russian, Polish, and Swedish forces during the Livonian Wars. In 1632, Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden established the University of Tartu, shaping the city's academic legacy.

18th - 19th Century

Russian Empire Period

Tartu became part of the Russian Empire after the Great Northern War and the university reopened in 1802. The city also played a significant role in the Estonian national awakening during this time.

20th Century

Independence and World War I

After Estonia declared independence in 1918, Tartu became a key cultural and academic center. The Tartu Peace Treaty of 1920 was signed here, confirming Estonia's independence from Soviet Russia.

1940 - 1991

World War II and Soviet Era

Tartu suffered severe destruction during World War II, with bombings from both Soviet and German forces. Under Soviet occupation, Tartu became part of the Estonian SSR, with restricted academic and cultural freedom.

Post-1991

Modern Independence

After Estonia regained independence in 1991, Tartu quickly reestablished itself as an intellectual and cultural hub. The University of Tartu became central to this revival, attracting students and researchers globally.

Present

Present Day

Today, Tartu is recognized as Estonia's intellectual capital, known for its academic excellence and cultural vibrancy. The city hosts prominent events like sTARTUp Day, fostering innovation and entrepreneurship.

Notable People from Tartu

Markus Villig (born: 1994) is the founder and CEO of Bolt (formerly Taxify), a major European ride-hailing and mobility platform.

Ahti Heinla (born: 1972) is a co-founder of Skype, bringing global recognition to Estonia's tech sector.

Taavet Hinrikus (born: February 11, 1981) is the co-founder of Wise (formerly TransferWise), revolutionizing international money transfers.

Ludvig Puuseppr (1875 - 1942) was a pioneering neurosurgeon considered one of the founders of modern neurosurgery.
